How It Works

AT&T U-verse Voice uses Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) to convert your voice into data. Your calls are sent and received over your High Speed Internet connection, but you can keep your phones – they'll work just fine. You can keep the phone number you already have or request a new one.

Advanced Features

U-verse Voice gives you all your favorite standard home phone features like Voicemail, Call Waiting, and Call Forwarding. In addition, you’ll get great advanced features like:

  • Call Screening – Only accept calls from a list of phone numbers you select.
  • Call Transfer – Send a call that’s already in progress to a different phone number.
  • Locate Me - Provides simultaneous ringing on up to four wireless/landline numbers when someone calls your home phone.

If you’ve bundled U-verse Voice with U-verse TV, you’ll also get great interactive features like:

  • Call History – View a list of your recent calls, by date and time, either online or on your TV screen.
  • Click to Call – Return a phone call using your U-verse TV screen and remote.
  • Caller ID on TV – See Caller ID and Voice Mail notifications on your TV screen.

Wireless Gateway

The AT&T U-verse Wireless Gateway is an intelligent device that brings together all your U-verse TV, Internet, and Voice services and distributes them throughout your home. You may choose to hook up your U-verse Voice service by:
•    Plug and Play – Connect one phone directly into the Wireless Gateway, enabling only a single wall jack to continue to work.
•    Use Existing Phone Jacks – Allows for the use of all the wall jacks in your home. An existing phone jack must be located near the Gateway.

Battery Backup

AT&T U-verse Voice, like other Voice over Internet Protocol ("Voice over IP" or "VoIP") services, requires the electrical power in your home to operate. Depending on your service address, your AT&T U-verse Voice service will include either a Residential Gateway Battery Backup Unit ("BBU") or an iNID Power Supply Unit ("iPSU").

Phone

You can use your regular corded or cordless phones with U-verse Voice; you don’t need to buy a special digital phone.


U-verse Voice Installation

Do you qualify for U-verse Voice Self Installation?

If you already have U-verse TV or High Speed Internet service, then you’ve got what you need to install U-verse Voice yourself. However, if you have a monitored home alarm or don’t already have U-verse TV or High Speed Internet, you’ll need professional installation by an AT&T Technician.

Getting Started

If you're eligible for self installation, an installation kit will be shipped to your home address (shipping & handling charge applies). The installation kit will contain all of the necessary equipment and access to our Online Interactive Assistant for easy, step-by-step instructions.

Installation Options

Depending on the layout of your home, two installation options are available.

Option 1: Plug and Play capability

Option 2: Use Existing Phone Jacks

Please do not attempt installation of U-verse Voice until your installation due date. You’ll receive your installation due date when you place your order or in your U-verse Voice Welcome e-mail.

Connecting your Battery Backup Unit

Turn off your Gateway: Power down your Gateway by unplugging the Power Adapter. You will no longer need this power supply.

Add the Battery Backup Unit: Connect the Battery Backup by plugging the round connector into the Power port on the Gateway. Then connect the electrical plug to an electrical outlet. Switch the power to On. You should see a blinking then steady green Power light to indicate a successful connection.

Emergency/Terminal Backup

U-verse Voice, like all Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) services, requires the electrical power in your home to operate. In case of power outage, U-verse Voice includes a Wireless Gateway Battery Backup unit with an initial backup battery.

This backup unit provides approximately 4 hours of battery backup time with a new, fully charged battery. So when the power goes out, your voice won’t.
